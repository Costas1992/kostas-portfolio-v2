# Customer Loyalty Classifier

## The Problem

Running a servise business means you see patterns — some clients come every three weeks like clockwork, others drift away and never come back. The challenge is knowing *which* clients are slipping before it's too late to act.

I decided to solve this with machine learning. Using real anonymized visit data from a salon (200 clients, 12 months), I built a system that automatically segments every client into one of three categories: **Loyal**, **At Risk**, or **Lost**.

---

## The Approach — RFM Analysis

RFM is a classic marketing model that scores customers on three dimensions:

- **Recency** — How recently did they visit?
- **Frequency** — How often do they come?
- **Monetary** — How much do they spend (total visits as proxy)?

I calculated these three scores for every client, then used **KMeans clustering** to group them into 3 natural segments. The model doesn't just follow fixed rules — it finds the actual patterns in the data.

## Key Lessons

**Clustering is unsupervised** — there are no labeled answers to train from. You have to interpret the clusters yourself by looking at their average RFM scores and assigning meaningful labels.

**Scaling matters** — KMeans measures distance. Without StandardScaler, a feature like total visits (0–28) would dominate recency (1–12) just because the numbers are bigger.

**Real data is messy** — clients with zero visits across all months, inconsistent visit patterns, and edge cases all required careful handling before the model could work reliably.
